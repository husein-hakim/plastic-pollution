Disadvantages of Plastic Pollution

Plastic is incredibly useful because of its low cost of manufacture and cheap availability, but it has a lot of flaws, and its detrimental effects are a major source of concern for us as we try to rescue our planet and ourselves. The following are a few of its drawbacks:

  • Plastic can take 400-1000 years to decompose naturally, and some forms of plastic are non-degradable.
  • Plastics pollute canals, oceans, seas, and lakes, among other places. Marine litter has entangled one out of every three species of marine animals.
  • Many animals die after consuming plastic materials. Plastic fragments are found in the stomachs of almost 90% of all seabirds.
  • In packaging, plastic is commonly utilised. Eating meals from plastic containers has been linked to cancer.
  • Plastic production and recycling both emit hazardous gases and residues, polluting the air, water, and land.
  • A few compounds, such as phthalates, that are added to plastics to prevent their structure from breaking down, may cause major hormone imbalance in both males and females.
  • Many fire hazards are caused by plastic.
  • Its recycling costs are likewise very expensive.
